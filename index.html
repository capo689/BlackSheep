Our Product

Single-origin dark chocolate with a different flavor profile in every country.

We strive on making the best single-origin dark chocolate. We offer chocolate bars from different countries that come with their own unique flavor profile. All of our cacao beans are sourced via transparent trade. Our packaging is biodegradable and the inner sleeve is home-compostable.

We also offer cacao tea, made with 100% roasted cacao husks, and drinking chocolate made with a blend of our cacao origins.

Our Process

Harvested, fermented, dried, sorted, roasted, cracked, winnowed, ground, tempered, poured.

The beans are first harvested at the cacao farm and undergo a fermentation process. After fermentation, the beans are dried and shipped to a co-op. Once we receive the cacao beans, they are sorted and roasted. Roasting helps the beans develop their flavor and removes any acid remaining from fermentation process.

After roasting, the beans are cracked and winnowed to remove the shells, and nibs of cacao are left. The next step is the melanger, a machine that grinds the beans down to small particles that give a smoother texture. Next, the now liquid chocolate is tempered and poured into molds. After cooling, the new bars are packaged and ready to enjoy.

Our Story

Born in Bend. Rebuilt in Stoughton. Still family-run.

For over ten years, founder Tom Seeley perfected the chocolate process starting with small batches at his home in Bend, OR for friends and family. Black Sheep Chocolate was officially born in 2019. Tom brought on his nephew Eric Wojta and his wife Rachel in 2021. Rachel and Eric became owners in 2022, moving the company and production from Bend to Stoughton, WI.
